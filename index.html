About me

I am a French PhD student currently working at the Institut de Recherche en Astrophysique et Planétologie (IRAP), located at the Observatoire Midi Pyrénée (OMP) in Toulouse, France. My research focuses on the modeling of M dwarfs spectra and their comparison to data recorded with SPIRou, in a project funded by the European Research Council [grant #740651, NEWWORLDS].

Prior to this, I obtained a master's degree in fundamental physics from the Aix-Marseille University, with a specialization in particles and astroparticles, before focusing on the study of planets and stars.

When I am not doing science, you will find me playing music, reading, fixing my bike or running.

Reasearch

My research focuses on the study of M dwarfs: these somewhat small reddish objects that are the most numerous stars around us. Because these objects are brighter in the near-infrared (nIR), several instruments were developped in the recent years to specifically observe these targets at high resolution. This is the case of SPIRou, a high-resolution near-infrared spectro-polarimeter, installed at the Canadian French Hawaii Telescope (CFHT).

Because they are so numerous, M dwarfs have attracted increasing interest in the hunt for exoplanets in the habitable zone, but detecting and characterizing planets orbiting these stars remains a great technical challenge. In particular, to derive the masses or radii of the planets, it is essential to know the host stars as well as possible: this is what we do, by analyzing the spectra of M dwarfs recorded with SPIRou, using state of the art synthetic models.
